Crocheted Chatter

Name Origin

The word crochet comes from the French word crochet, which derives from the word croche. Croche is derived from the Germanic word croc, meaning hook.

History

Some of the first substantive evidence of crocheted fabric dates back to Europe in the 19th century. The exact origins of crochet are debated. Some believe that it's roots trace back to the Arab trade route, originating in Arabia, before spreading to Tibet, Spain, and other Mediterranean countries. Another theory places the origin in South America where it was used for embellishment. The last possible origin is traced back to China, where dolls were worked entirely in crochet. The first known published instructions, for crochet, were found in a Dutch magazine in 1823. In the magazine, a shepherd's hook is described, and illustrated, as a 19th century crochet hook, made of steel, ivory, or box-wood.

Early Example

Irish crochet was introduced in the 19th century during the Great Irish Famine, as a form of famine relief. Also the production of crocheted lace served as a way of making money for poor Irish workers. Men, women, and children joined to produce items to help with the famine. Schools even joined and started to train teachers to teach. Mademoiselle Riego de la Branchardiere is credited with inventing Irish Crochet.
